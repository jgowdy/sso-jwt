# Code of Conduct

## Scope

This project is a technical collaboration space. This Code of Conduct applies
to all interactions in project-controlled environments (e.g., repositories,
issues, discussions, and related communication channels).

## Expected Conduct

Participants are expected to:

- Keep discussions technical, relevant, and professional
- Focus on code, architecture, performance, and correctness
- Provide clear, actionable feedback
- Disagree on ideas without escalating into personal disputes

## Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviors are not permitted:

- Credible threats of violence
- Doxxing or sharing private personal information
- Targeted harassment (repeated, unwanted personal attacks toward an individual)
- Spam or deliberate disruption of project activities
- Impersonation or misrepresentation of identity for deceptive purposes

## Not a Violation

The following are explicitly allowed:

- Direct, blunt, or critical technical feedback
- Strong disagreement on design, implementation, or direction
- Rejection of proposals without obligation to provide extensive justification
- Discussions that are strictly technical but may be perceived as harsh

## Enforcement

Project maintainers have sole discretion to:

- Remove content that violates this policy
- Lock or close discussions
- Restrict or remove access to project resources

Enforcement decisions are final.

## Reporting

Violations can be reported to project maintainers through project-defined
channels. Reports should include specific evidence (links, logs, or
screenshots).

## Liability and Intent

This Code of Conduct is intended to:

- Protect contributors and maintainers from disruptive or abusive behavior
- Provide clear, objective standards for participation
- Maintain a functional engineering environment

It is not intended to regulate tone beyond what is necessary to prevent the
prohibited conduct listed above.
